Yes, generic medicines contain the same active ingredients and undergo strict quality checks to ensure they are just as effective. The FDA requires generic drugs to be bioequivalent to brand-name drugs, meaning they work in the same way and provide the same clinical benefit.

Generic medicines are cheaper because they do not require extensive research and marketing costs like branded medicines. When a company develops a new drug, they spend significant resources on research, development, and clinical trials. Generic manufacturers can avoid these costs because the drug's safety and efficacy have already been established. Additionally, market competition among multiple generic manufacturers helps lower prices.
